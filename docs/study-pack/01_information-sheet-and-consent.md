## Part A — Participant Information Sheet

**Study title:** Does learning a concept through an interactive game *before* self-assessment
improve understanding? A study of the COMPGame flipped-learning platform.

**Researcher:** [Student name], BSc (Hons) [programme], The Hong Kong Polytechnic University.
Final-Year Project, COMP3423 Human–Computer Interaction.
**Supervisor:** [Supervisor name], Department of Computing, PolyU.
**Contact:** [researcher email] · Supervisor: [supervisor email]

### What is this study about?
COMPGame teaches HCI concepts by letting you first explore a concept in a short interactive
game (the *Understanding* game), then test yourself in an *Assessment* game, with an AI tutor
available throughout. We want to find out whether this "learn-by-playing-first" approach helps
students understand HCI concepts, how motivating and satisfying they find it, and how they
experience the game and AI tutor as a teacher. Your answers help us improve the platform and
form part of an academic project.

### What will I be asked to do?
If you agree to take part, in a single sitting of about **45–60 minutes** you will:
1. Answer a few background questions (age range, year of study, prior experience — no name needed).
2. For each of four HCI topics: answer a short multiple-choice quiz, play a short Understanding
   game and an Assessment game on the platform, answer a second short quiz, and rate how much
   mental effort the topic took.
3. Spend a few minutes with the AI tutor.
4. Write brief reflections and complete short questionnaires about your motivation, your
   experience of the game and AI tutor, and your satisfaction.

There are no right-or-wrong "grades" that affect your studies. The quizzes only measure how the
materials are working.

### Do I have to take part?
No. Participation is **entirely voluntary**. You may decline to answer any question, take a
break, or stop and withdraw at any time, for any reason, without giving a reason and without any
penalty or effect on your course standing, marks, or relationship with the department. If you
withdraw during the session, you can ask that your responses so far be discarded.

### Are there any risks or benefits?
There are no foreseeable risks beyond the mild effort of doing quizzes and using a computer. You
may find the games an enjoyable way to revise HCI concepts. There is no payment for taking part.

### How is my data handled?
- The study is **anonymous**. We do **not** collect your name. You will use a participant code
  (e.g., `P01`) or your student ID is replaced by a code before analysis; no one outside the
  research team can link responses back to you.
- Responses are stored as de-identified data on password-protected, PolyU-associated storage and
  used only for this project (FYP report, possible academic paper, and platform improvement).
- Only the researcher and supervisor have access. Aggregated, non-identifying results may be
  reported; no individual will be identifiable in any output.
- Data are retained for the period required by PolyU FYP regulations, then securely deleted.

### Who has approved this study?
This project follows the ethical guidelines of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University.
If you have questions about your rights as a participant, contact [supervisor name / department
research-ethics contact].

### Questions?
Ask the researcher now, or contact [researcher email] at any time.
